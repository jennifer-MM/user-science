Usability analysis

Focused mobile UX

Posted by Jennifer Menard on May 3, 2020

Introduction

I transitioned from last week's usability study on JoAnn.com, desktop version, to its mobile site.

JoAnn.com is an e-commerce business, providing visitors the opportunity to purchase fabric and craft supplies. The mobile site provides an informational section to inspire visitors to try new craft projects, promote sales, and feature products.

I conducted a virtual usability test using a live version of JoAnn.com using Zoom, a video conferencing platform. The session captured each participant's navigational choices, task completion rates, comments, and overall satisfaction rating, questions and feedback.

Executive Summary

The virtual usability test assesses the usability of the web interface design, information flow, and information architecture. It was conducted on Friday, May 1, 2020 and Saturday, May 2, 2020. Three people participated in the test during their own session. Test scenarios were the same over the two days.

The purpose of the test is to determine whether the website is easy to use. This report is a combination of the results and recommendations from both sets of users. The test identified problems including:

  • Lack of capability to search individual stores' stock
  • Confusion of the process to order online and pick up at local store
  • COVID-19 pop up distracts users
  • Overwhelming amount of information and images
  • The pages are too long without having a "scroll up" feature-

The information contained here includes the participant feedback, task completion rates, ease or difficulty of completion rating, errors and recommendations for improvement. A copy of the instructions and task scenarios are included at the end of the report.

Methodology

The user tests were conducted over two days. Each session was approximately 10 minutes in length. Participants were asked to complete three tasks. Post-scenario measures included:

  • How easy it was to find the information
  • How confident were you that you knew which section of the website contained the information you needed
  • Ability to keep track of their location

Participants

I recruited three participants for the usability test. All three woman are in the 30-56 years-old age range. All three have previously shopped inside a JoAnn's store, but have not previously used the website on a mobile device.

Evaluation Tasks and Scenarios

Test task: Buy an assorted package of acrylic paints, that are available to pick up at the store.

Test scenario: You want to avoid going into JoAnn’s tomorrow but need acrylic paint for a project. You decide to us the store’s drive-up feature. Find a multi-pack of paints that are available for pick up at your local JoAnn store.

Test task: Buy 12 ft. of fabric

Test scenario: You’re making a blanket for your son. His favorite superhero is Spiderman. Find a Spiderman fabric and add it to your cart.

Test task: Buy a cake pan

Test Task You’re baking a sheet cake next week and need a new pan. Find a the top rated 9x13 cake pan and add it to your cart.

Results

Two participants were able to locate a package of acrylic paints that were available at their local store. The third participant, with experience using mobile applications, not shopping using a website on a mobile device, was uable to navigate to the "Paint" inventory.

All participants completed Task 2 and 3. Every participant expressed frustration with scrolling through the inventory.

Overall Ratings

After the completion of the test, participants rated the overall site using the 5-point rating scale from 1 (strongly disagree) to 5 (strongly agree).

Recommendations

Users want to see changes that will make it easier to navigate and reduce the visual distractions on the homepage.

  1. Create a separate mobile version of JoAnn.com and prioritize the information and products mobile users want. When everything is important, than nothing is important.
  2. Remove the COVID-19 information pop up from appearing every time a new page loads. It's a help resource for customers that only needs to appear once throughout their visit.
  3. Add the capability to search local stores' inventory.
  4. The pages are very long. Its frustrating to navigate. The site should give users the opportunity to "scroll up" quickly and have access to the navigation bar without having to scroll all the way to the top.
