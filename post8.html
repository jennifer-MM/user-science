Setting up Events in Google Analytics

Posted by Jennifer Menard on May 31, 2020

My goal is to learn how and what people are engaging with while visiting my site. I set up three goals in Google Analytics-- to help me understand what people are and are not doing while visiting my site. I created a new page titled, Font Resouces, that links to open source fonts--

Google Analytic goals are different user actions that can be measured. I choose two specific-action goals and one to track the amount of page views per visit.

My goals are:

  1. Clicking "Font Resources" link on homepage (EVENT)
  2. Downloading one of the four fonts on the Font Resources page (EVENT)
  3. Visiting more than two pages during a visit (PAGE VIEWS)

My goals are accessible to visitors. The first goal is a link on the homepage-- that users would have to click. The second goal requires most users to click two different links to-- to have the opportunity to download a font. I assume most visitors will travel to my site through a posting or the homepage. On both pages, the users would have to click "Font Resources" to get to the page. is a link- users would have to click to download the font.

Completing the technical aspect of setting up the goals in Google Analytics is straight forward. The site provides an informational step-by-step format to include templates.
